Lemniscate of Bernoulli Visualizer

Overview

This visualization explores the Lemniscate of Bernoulli given by the polar equation r = a sqrt(cos 2θ).

Because the expression under the square root must be nonnegative, the curve only exists where cos 2θ ≥ 0. The black dotted boundaries show where cos 2θ = 0. Near those boundaries, the curve becomes nearly tangent to boundary directions.

Interactive Graph

Press play on t to watch the lemniscate be drawn. The reference line shows the current direction of θ.

Why the Curve Nearly Aligns with the Boundary

The graph below shows y = sqrt(x) near the origin. The graph of y = sqrt(x) is the upper half of the sideways parabola x = y2. Its vertex is at the origin, and near that vertex the curve has a vertical tangent. So when we zoom into the origin, the curve becomes almost indistinguishable from the vertical line x = 0.

The lemniscate a sqrt(cos 2θ) has a similar square-root behavior near the dotted boundary lines. If we think of u = cos 2θ, then the boundaries occur when u = 0. When u ≈ 0, the expression r = a sqrt(u) behaves like y = sqrt(x) near x = 0. The lemniscate nearly aligns with the boundary direction.
