Partial Derivative Slice Visualizer

This visualization shows how a partial derivative measures the slope of a two-variable function in one direction at a time. The turquoise surface represents z = f(x,y).

To understand ∂f/∂y, we keep x = a fixed and only allow y to change. The orange curve is the slice of the surface along the plane x = a, and the black point P = (a, b, f(a,b)) is the point where the partial derivative is being measured.

The green vector represents a small nudge Δy in the y-direction. The red vector shows the corresponding change in height Δf, using Δf ≈ (∂f/∂y)(a,b)Δy. Together, these form the purple tangent direction vector.

(Note: the value of "Δy" only changes the apparent length of the vectors. The actual partial derivative (∂f/∂y)(a,b) is the slope of the purple vector Δz/Δy in this tangent direction)
