Eccentricity: Ellipse vs. Hyperbola

This visualization shows how a centered conic changes as c, the distance from the origin to each focus, changes relative to a. The value e = c/a is the eccentricity, so increasing c also increases the eccentricity.

When c < a, the graph is an ellipse. In this case, the foci lie inside the ellipse, and the relationship between the parameters is a² = b² + c². As c gets larger, the ellipse becomes more stretched out and less circle-like.

When c > a, the graph becomes a hyperbola. In this case, the eccentricity e is greater than 1, and the foci lie outside the central rectangle. The relationship between the parameters changes to c² = a² + b².

The red points mark the vertices at (±a, 0); connecting them gives the transverse axis for the hyperbola and the major axis for the ellipse. The blue points mark (0, ±b); connecting them gives the conjugate axis for the hyperbola and the minor axis for the ellipse. The purple points mark the foci at (±c, 0). The dashed orange lines show the hyperbola's asymptotes, and the dotted green rectangle shows the ellipse's bounding box or the hyperbola's central rectangle.
